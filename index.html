End-Effector-Elbow: A New Action Space
for Robot Learning

Anonymous authors

Abstract

Joint control and end-effector control are the two most dominant control methods for robot arms within the robot learning literature. Joint control, while precise, often suffers from inefficient training; end-effector control boasts data-efficient training but sacrifices the ability to perform tasks in confined spaces due to limited control the robot joint configuration. This paper introduces a novel action space formulation: End-Effector-Elbow (E3), which addresses the limitations of existing control paradigms by allowing the control of both the end-effector and elbow pose of the robot. E3 combines the advantages of both joint and end-effector control, offering fine-grained comprehensive control with overactuated robot arms whilst achieving high-efficiency robot learning. E3 systematically outperforms other action spaces, when precise control over the robot's configuration is required, both in simulated and real environments.
